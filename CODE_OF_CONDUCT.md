# Code of Conduct

This project is a scholarly and community-oriented resource list. Contributors are expected to collaborate respectfully, carefully, and in good faith.

## Expected Conduct

- Be respectful toward other contributors, maintainers, researchers, archivists, educators, and community members.
- Treat Holocaust testimony and oral-history materials with particular care.
- Avoid language that trivializes violence, trauma, persecution, displacement, antisemitism, racism, or other forms of harm.
- Respect survivors, witnesses, interviewees, families, communities, and archival contexts.
- Make evidence-based contributions and distinguish verified public information from uncertain leads.
- Follow archive terms of use, copyright restrictions, access rules, and ethical expectations.

## Unacceptable Conduct

- Harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language, or intimidation.
- Sharing restricted testimony content, personal data, or scraped archival material.
- Presenting unverified resources as open or reusable.
- Encouraging unauthorized model training on protected collections.
- Decontextualizing testimony material in ways that could mislead or harm.

## Reporting and Response

If you see a problem, please open an issue or contact the repository maintainer. Maintainers may edit, reject, or remove contributions that violate this code, expose sensitive material, or create legal or ethical risk.

The goal is to make this list useful while preserving trust, dignity, and care around sensitive historical and personal materials.
